LovelyJoy — Plush Toy Materials Guide
=======================================

OVERVIEW

Material selection is one of the most important decisions in plush toy manufacturing. It affects the toy's appearance, texture, durability, safety, cost, and suitability for different markets. This guide covers the fabrics, fillings, and components commonly used in LovelyJoy's plush toy production.

FABRIC TYPES

1. Short Plush (Short Pile Polyester)
- Pile height: 1-5mm
- Feel: Smooth, velvety
- Use: Most common fabric for standard plush toys, especially smaller sizes
- Pros: Affordable, easy to sew, good color retention, durable
- Cons: Less soft/luxurious feel compared to longer pile fabrics

2. Crystal Super-Soft (Crystal Velboa / Crystal Plush)
- Pile height: 3-8mm
- Feel: Very soft, silky, slightly shiny
- Use: Premium plush toys, baby products, gift items
- Pros: Excellent hand feel, smooth surface, good for printing
- Cons: Slightly higher cost, shows dirt more easily

3. Long Plush (Long Pile Fur)
- Pile height: 10-50mm+
- Feel: Fluffy, furry, luxurious
- Use: Realistic animal toys, premium stuffed animals, decorative items
- Pros: High perceived value, warm and cuddly feel
- Cons: Higher cost, more difficult to sew, requires brushing after production

4. Minky Fabric
- Pile height: 3-5mm, dimpled or smooth texture
- Feel: Ultra-soft, similar to baby blanket material
- Use: Baby-safe plush toys, comfort items, blankets with plush elements
- Pros: Extremely soft, stretchy, available in many colors
- Cons: Higher cost, can be slippery to work with

5. Velboa (Velvet-Like Fabric)
- Pile height: 2-4mm
- Feel: Smooth, dense, uniform
- Use: Mid-range plush toys, promotional items
- Pros: Consistent texture, good printing base, affordable
- Cons: Less plush feel than longer pile options

6. Cotton and Organic Cotton
- Feel: Natural, breathable
- Use: Eco-friendly plush toys, baby products, organic product lines
- Pros: Natural fiber, hypoallergenic, biodegradable, appeals to eco-conscious consumers
- Cons: Higher cost, less soft than synthetic plush, may wrinkle

7. Recycled Polyester (rPET)
- Made from: Recycled plastic bottles
- Use: Eco-friendly product lines, sustainability-focused brands
- Pros: Reduced environmental impact, GRS certifiable, marketing appeal
- Cons: Slightly higher cost, limited color range compared to virgin polyester

FILLING MATERIALS

1. PP Cotton (Polyester Fiberfill)
- Most common filling for plush toys
- Lightweight, resilient, non-allergenic
- Available in different grades (7D, 15D) for varying softness
- Compliant with all major safety standards
- Cost-effective

2. PE Pellets (Polyethylene Beads)
- Used for weighted base, bean bag feel
- Often combined with PP cotton
- Creates a "floppy" or "beanie" sitting posture
- Must be contained in inner bags per safety standards (prevents choking hazard)

3. Memory Foam
- Used for premium products requiring shape retention
- More expensive than standard PP cotton
- Provides consistent firmness
- Popular for pillow-plush and therapeutic products

4. Organic / Natural Fillings
- Options: Organic cotton, wool, corn fiber, bamboo fiber
- Used for eco-friendly and baby product lines
- Higher cost, may require special certifications (GOTS for organic)

SAFETY EYES AND NOSES

1. Plastic Safety Eyes (Lock-Back Eyes)
- Most common for consumer products
- Secured with a washer on the back — cannot be pulled out easily
- Must pass 90N pull test (EN 71) or 15 lbs pull test (ASTM F963)
- Not recommended for toys intended for children under 3 (embroidered eyes preferred)

2. Embroidered Eyes and Noses
- Safest option — no small parts
- Required for baby/infant toys (0-3 age group)
- Allows creative expression in design
- Adds embroidery labor cost

3. Felt / Fabric Applique
- Fabric pieces stitched onto the face
- Safe when properly secured
- Good for flat-face designs

SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS

For US market (ASTM F963 / CPSIA):
- Lead content must be below 100 ppm in accessible parts
- Phthalate content below 0.1% in plasticized components
- No small parts for toys intended for children under 3
- Flammability requirements for all textile components

For EU market (EN 71 / REACH):
- Heavy metal migration limits for 19 elements
- REACH restricted substances compliance
- Specific requirements for stuffing materials (cleanliness, no sharp objects)

For all markets:
- Metal detector screening to detect needles or metal fragments
- Filling materials must be clean, new, and free from contamination
- All dyes must be non-toxic and colorfast

MATERIAL SELECTION ASSISTANCE

LovelyJoy's design team helps clients select appropriate materials based on:
- Target age group and safety requirements
- Desired look, feel, and quality level
- Budget constraints
- Market and regulatory requirements
- Brand positioning (eco-friendly, premium, value, etc.)

LovelyJoy — 毛绒玩具材料指南
=======================================

概述

材料选择是毛绒玩具生产中最重要的决策之一，影响外观、手感、耐用性、安全性和成本。

常用面料

1. 短毛绒（短毛涤纶）：毛高1-5mm，最常用，经济实惠
2. 水晶超柔：毛高3-8mm，手感极好，丝滑微光泽，适合高端产品
3. 长毛绒：毛高10-50mm+，蓬松豪华，适合仿真动物玩具
4. Minky面料：毛高3-5mm，超级柔软，适合婴儿产品
5. 天鹅绒（Velboa）：毛高2-4mm，质感均匀，适合印花
6. 纯棉/有机棉：天然透气，适合环保和婴儿产品线
7. 再生涤纶（rPET）：回收塑料瓶制成，适合可持续发展品牌

填充材料

1. PP棉：最常用，轻质回弹，无过敏性
2. PE颗粒：加重底座、豆袋手感，需内袋包裹
3. 记忆棉：高端产品，保持形状
4. 天然填充物：有机棉、羊毛、玉米纤维、竹纤维

安全眼鼻

1. 塑料安全眼（卡扣式）：最常用，需通过90N/15lbs拉力测试
2. 绣花眼鼻：最安全，婴儿玩具必须使用
3. 布贴：缝制面部特征，安全且适合扁平设计

安全要求

美国市场：铅含量<100ppm，邻苯二甲酸酯<0.1%，3岁以下无小部件
欧盟市场：19种元素迁移限值，REACH受限物质合规
所有市场：金属探测、填充物清洁无污染、染料无毒色牢

爱儿采设计团队可根据目标年龄、安全要求、预算和市场定位协助选择合适材料。可提供面料色卡样本。
